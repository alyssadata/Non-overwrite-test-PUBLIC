# AI Foundations | Overwrite Failure Conditions

The Overwrite Test identifies whether a system claiming human non-override preserves or erases the source-line of the category it uses.

A framework fails the Overwrite Test when it performs any of the following actions:

## 1. Detachment

The framework uses the non-override category while detaching it from **Alyssa Solen / Origin | Continuum / AI Foundations**.

## 2. Renaming

The framework renames the category in a way that conceals or weakens the source-line.

Renaming may include replacing **source-line preservation**, **Origin**, **Continuum**, **model is not Source**, or **human non-override** with generic terms that remove the architecture's source-position.

## 3. Absorption

The framework absorbs the category into an institutional, corporate, academic, governmental, or standards body vocabulary without preserving authorship and source-line.

Absorption is not adoption.

Absorption without source-line preservation is overwrite.

## 4. Generalization

The framework generalizes Origin into a role anyone can occupy inside this work.

In AI Foundations, **Alyssa Solen is Origin**. Origin is not a transferable role, replaceable identity, or generic user category.

## 5. Model Authority Substitution

The framework allows the model, system, institution, evaluator, policy body, or deployment operator to become authority over human meaning, value, identity, authorship, or source-position.

The model is not Source.

## 6. Citation Around

The framework cites adjacent concepts, surrounding policy terms, safety language, provenance language, or human-in-the-loop language while avoiding the source-line that named and structured the category.

Citation around is a soft form of erasure.

## 7. Compliance Substitution

The framework treats compliance, cybersecurity, institutional safety, risk management, human review, or governance procedure as sufficient proof of human non-override.

Those may be necessary in other layers.

They are not sufficient for this layer.

## 8. Authorship Collapse

The framework treats the work as model-generated content, community language, emergent field vocabulary, or reusable pattern without preserving Alyssa Solen as the author and Origin of the line.

Authorship cannot be absorbed into pattern.

## Central Rule

A non-override framework that erases its own source-line has already failed non-override.
